Order of Operations & Negative Numbers

AQA GCSE — Number (N1, N2) — Maths Genie 1.8 & 1.10

BIDMAS

BIDMAS (also written BODMAS) tells you the order in which to carry out operations in any calculation. Always follow this sequence:

Letter Meaning Example
BBrackets(3 + 2) × 4 → brackets first → 5 × 4
IIndices (powers & roots)3² + 1 → 9 + 1
D / MDivision and Multiplication (left → right)8 ÷ 4 × 2 → 2 × 2 → 4 (not 1!)
A / SAddition and Subtraction (left → right)10 − 3 + 2 → 7 + 2 → 9 (not 5!)

⚠ Division and Multiplication have equal priority — work left to right. Same for Addition and Subtraction.

BIDMAS with Brackets and Powers

Example: Calculate 3 + 2 × (7 − 4)²

  1. Brackets: (7 − 4) = 3
  2. Indices: 3² = 9
  3. Multiply: 2 × 9 = 18
  4. Add: 3 + 18 = 21

Example: Calculate (2 + 3)² ÷ 5 − 1

  1. Brackets: (2 + 3) = 5
  2. Indices: 5² = 25
  3. Divide: 25 ÷ 5 = 5
  4. Subtract: 5 − 1 = 4

Negative Numbers — Key Rules

You must know the sign rules for all four operations:

Addition and Subtraction

Think of a number line. Adding a negative is the same as subtracting; subtracting a negative is the same as adding.

  • 5 + (−3) = 5 − 3 = 2
  • 5 − (−3) = 5 + 3 = 8
  • −4 + (−2) = −4 − 2 = −6

Multiplication and Division

SignsResultExample
+ × ++3 × 4 = 12
− × −+(−3) × (−4) = 12
+ × −−3 × (−4) = −12
− × +−(−3) × 4 = −12

Same rules apply to division: same signs → positive, different signs → negative.

Negative Numbers with Powers

Be very careful with brackets when squaring negative numbers.

  • (−3)² = (−3) × (−3) = +9 — the negative is inside the brackets, so it gets squared
  • −(3²) = −(3 × 3) = −9 — only 3 is squared; the negative sign stays outside

⚠ This is one of the most common errors in GCSE exams. Always check whether the negative is inside or outside the brackets before squaring.

Combining BIDMAS with Negatives

Example: Calculate −2 × (3 + 5) − (−4)²

  1. Brackets: (3 + 5) = 8 and (−4)² is computed next
  2. Indices: (−4)² = +16
  3. Multiply: −2 × 8 = −16
  4. Subtract: −16 − 16 = −32

Example: Calculate (−3)² + 4 × (−2)

  1. Indices: (−3)² = 9
  2. Multiply: 4 × (−2) = −8
  3. Add: 9 + (−8) = 1

Quick Reference

  • Brackets → Indices → Division/Multiplication (left→right) → Addition/Subtraction (left→right)
  • Subtracting a negative = adding: a − (−b) = a + b
  • Same signs multiply/divide to give a positive
  • Different signs multiply/divide to give a negative
  • (−n)² = positive  |  −(n²) = negative
