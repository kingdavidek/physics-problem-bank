1 Understanding Ratio All Exam Boards Year 7

A ratio tells you how two (or more) amounts compare. It uses a colon, like a score in sport: 3 : 2 means "3 for every 2".

Example: A bag has red and blue counters in the ratio 3 : 2. That means for every 3 red counters, there are 2 blue counters.

We can count the total parts. Here that's 3 + 2 = 5 parts altogether. So red is \(\frac{3}{5}\) of all the counters and blue is \(\frac{2}{5}\).

Exam tip: Write ratios in their simplest form, like you simplify fractions. The ratio 12 : 8 becomes 3 : 2 when you divide both parts by 4.

🧠 Quick Check

Red and blue counters are in the ratio 3 : 2. How many equal parts are there in total?

2 Simplifying and Equivalent Ratios All Exam Boards Year 7

Example: Simplify 18 : 24.

Both numbers divide by 6. So 18 ÷ 6 = 3 and 24 ÷ 6 = 4. The simplified ratio is 3 : 4.

Equivalent ratios are different ways of writing the same comparison. You multiply or divide every part by the same number:

  • 2 : 5 → multiply both by 2 → 4 : 10
  • 2 : 5 → multiply both by 5 → 10 : 25

So 2 : 5, 4 : 10 and 10 : 25 all mean the same thing.

Common mistake: Don't add the same number to both sides. 2 : 5 is not the same as 3 : 6 — the multipliers are different (×1.5 vs ×1.2).

Simplify the ratio 18 : 24.

3 Sharing in a Ratio All Exam Boards Year 8

When you share money or items in a ratio, follow these steps every time:

  1. Add up the parts to get the total number of parts.
  2. Divide the whole amount by that total — that's the value of one part.
  3. Multiply one part by each number in the ratio.

Example: Share £60 in the ratio 2 : 3.

  • Total parts: 2 + 3 = 5
  • One part: £60 ÷ 5 = £12
  • First share: 2 × £12 = £24
  • Second share: 3 × £12 = £36

\( £60 \text{ in the ratio } 2:3 = £24:£36 \)

Exam tip: Add your two answers at the end. £24 + £36 = £60 ✓ — if they don't add back to the total, something went wrong.

🧠 Quick Check

Share £60 in the ratio 2 : 3. What is the smaller share?

4 Ratio and Fractions All Exam Boards Year 8

Example: In a class, boys : girls = 4 : 5.

Total parts = 4 + 5 = 9. So boys are \(\frac{4}{9}\) of the class and girls are \(\frac{5}{9}\).

If there are 72 students in total:

  • Boys = \(\frac{4}{9} \times 72 = 4 \times 8 =\) 32
  • Girls = \(\frac{5}{9} \times 72 = 5 \times 8 =\) 40

Check: 32 + 40 = 72 ✓

The general rule: if the ratio is \(a : b\), the first quantity is \(\frac{a}{a+b}\) of the whole and the second is \(\frac{b}{a+b}\).

🧠 Quick Check

Boys : girls = 4 : 5. There are 72 students. How many are boys?

5 Direct Proportion All Exam Boards Year 9

Two things are in direct proportion when they go up and down together by the same multiplier. Buy twice as many, pay twice as much.

Example: 4 notebooks cost £6. How much do 7 notebooks cost?

  1. Find the cost of 1 notebook: £6 ÷ 4 = £1.50
  2. Multiply for 7: 7 × £1.50 = £10.50

\(4 \text{ items cost } £6 \Rightarrow 1 \text{ item costs } £1.50 \Rightarrow 7 \text{ items cost } £10.50\)

We call this the unitary method: find the value for one unit first, then scale up (or down).

🧠 Quick Check

4 notebooks cost £6. How much do 7 notebooks cost?

6 Inverse Proportion All Exam Boards Year 10 Higher

In inverse proportion, when one quantity goes up, the other goes down. More workers on a job usually means less time to finish it.

Example: 4 workers take 9 hours. How long would 6 workers take?

  1. Find the total "work" (worker-hours): 4 × 9 = 36
  2. Share that total among 6 workers: 36 ÷ 6 = 6 hours

Exam tip: For workers, machines or speed questions, keep the total work (or total distance) fixed. Multiply to find it, then divide.

🧠 Quick Check

4 workers take 9 hours. How long would 6 workers take?

7 Scale Drawings and Maps All Exam Boards Year 9

A scale is a ratio that links a drawing to real life.

Example: Scale 1 : 50 means 1 cm on the drawing = 50 cm in real life.

Example: A map scale of 1 : 25,000 means 1 cm on the map = 25,000 cm in real life.

  • 25,000 cm = 250 m (÷ 100)
  • So 1 cm on the map = 250 m in real life

Common mistake: Watch your units. 100 cm = 1 m and 1000 m = 1 km. Convert before you multiply or divide.

🧠 Quick Check

On a map with scale 1 : 25,000, how far is 1 cm on the map in real life?

8 Best Buys and Recipes All Exam Boards Year 9 Problem Solving

Exams often test ratio in real-life situations — cooking and shopping are the big two.

Recipes: multiply every ingredient by the same scale factor.

Example: A recipe for 4 people uses 300 g flour. How much for 10 people?

  • Scale factor: 10 ÷ 4 = 2.5
  • Flour needed: 300 × 2.5 = 750 g

Best buys: work out the price for the same amount of each product — usually price per kg, per 100 g, or per item. Pick the smaller unit price.

Example: 500 g for £2.50 vs 750 g for £3.60. Price per 100 g: £0.50 vs £0.48 — the 750 g pack is better value.

🧠 Quick Check

A recipe for 4 people uses 300 g flour. How much flour for 10 people?
