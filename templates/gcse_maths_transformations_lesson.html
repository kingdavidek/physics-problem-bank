Transformations

A transformation moves or changes a shape on a grid. You will learn the four types — translation, reflection, rotation, and enlargement — and how to describe each one fully in exam answers.

1 What Are Transformations? All Exam Boards Year 8

Example: You slide a triangle 3 squares right and 1 square up. The triangle looks the same — just in a new place. That slide is a translation.

A transformation moves or changes a shape. The shape you start with is the object. The shape you get is the image.

At GCSE you need these four:

Transformation What changes What stays the same Result
TranslationPositionSize, shape, orientationCongruent
ReflectionPosition, orientationSize, shapeCongruent
RotationPosition, orientationSize, shapeCongruent
EnlargementPosition, sizeShape (angles)Similar

Isometry means lengths stay the same. Translations, reflections, and rotations are isometries. Enlargements with scale factor not equal to 1 (or −1) change size, so they are not.

In exams: When you describe a transformation, give all the details the examiner asks for (see each section below).

2 Translation All Exam Boards Year 9

Example: Point (2, 5) moves by vector \(\dbinom{3}{1}\). New position: (2+3, 5+1) = (5, 6) — 3 right, 1 up.

A translation slides a shape without turning or flipping it. We write it as a column vector:

\(\dbinom{a}{b}\) means move \(a\) units right (left if negative) and \(b\) units up (down if negative).

Every point moves the same way: \((x, y) \to (x+a,\; y+b)\).

To describe a translation in an exam: say "Translation by the vector \(\dbinom{a}{b}\)".

Key rule: \(\dbinom{3}{1}\) means \((x, y) \to (x+3,\; y+1)\).

Watch out: Top number = horizontal (x). Bottom number = vertical (y). Do not swap them.

3 Reflection All Exam Boards Year 9

Example: Reflect (3, 2) in the y-axis (\(x = 0\)). The x-coordinate flips sign: (−3, 2).

A reflection flips a shape in a mirror line. Each point ends up the same distance from the line, but on the other side.

To describe a reflection: say "Reflection in the line …" and give the equation (e.g. \(y = x\), \(x = 2\)).

Mirror line Rule Example
\(x\)-axis (\(y=0\))\((x,y)\to(x,-y)\)\((3,2)\to(3,-2)\)
\(y\)-axis (\(x=0\))\((x,y)\to(-x,y)\)\((3,2)\to(-3,2)\)
\(y=x\)\((x,y)\to(y,x)\)\((3,2)\to(2,3)\)
\(y=-x\)\((x,y)\to(-y,-x)\)\((3,2)\to(-2,-3)\)
\(x=k\)\((x,y)\to(2k-x,y)\)\((5,2)\) in \(x=3\) \(\to(1,2)\)
\(y=k\)\((x,y)\to(x,2k-y)\)\((3,5)\) in \(y=2\) \(\to(3,-1)\)
\(y=x+c\)\((x,y)\to(y-c,\,x+c)\)\((1,3)\) in \(y=x+1\) \(\to(2,2)\)

Invariant points: Points on the mirror line map to themselves. On the x-axis, every point \((a, 0)\) stays fixed.

4 Rotation All Exam Boards Year 10

Example: Rotate (2, 3) by 90° clockwise about the origin. Image: (3, −2) — use \((x,y) \to (y, -x)\).

A rotation turns a shape around a fixed point — the centre of rotation.

To describe a rotation you need three things: angle, direction (clockwise or anticlockwise), and centre.

Rotation about origin Rule Example
90° clockwise\((x,y)\to(y,-x)\)\((2,3)\to(3,-2)\)
90° anticlockwise\((x,y)\to(-y,x)\)\((2,3)\to(-3,2)\)
180°\((x,y)\to(-x,-y)\)\((2,3)\to(-2,-3)\)
270° CW (= 90° ACW)\((x,y)\to(-y,x)\)\((2,3)\to(-3,2)\)

Rotating about (a, b): Shift so the centre is at the origin, rotate, then shift back.

90° CW about \((a,b)\): \((x,y)\to(a+(y-b),\; b-(x-a))\)

Finding the centre: Join a point to its image and draw the perpendicular bisector. Do the same for another pair. Where the bisectors cross is the centre.

5 Enlargement All Exam Boards Year 10

Example: Enlarge a shape with area 5 cm² by scale factor 3 from the origin. Each length × 3, so area × \(3^2 = 9\). New area = 45 cm².

An enlargement makes a shape bigger or smaller from a fixed centre of enlargement. The scale factor \(k\) tells you how many times longer each side becomes.

About centre \((a, b)\) with scale factor \(k\):

\((x, y) \;\to\; \bigl(a + k(x-a),\;\; b + k(y-b)\bigr)\)

Scale factor \(k\) Effect Image size
\(k > 1\)Same side of centre, largerBigger
\(0 < k < 1\)Same side of centre, smallerSmaller
\(k = -1\)Opposite side, same sizeSame size (180° rotation)
\(k < -1\)Opposite side, largerBigger, inverted
\(-1 < k < 0\)Opposite side, smallerSmaller, inverted

Area rule: If lengths scale by \(k\), area scales by \(k^2\).

\(\text{Area of image} = k^2 \times \text{Area of object}\)

To describe an enlargement: "Enlargement, scale factor \(k\), centre \((a,b)\)".

Tip: Lines through matching vertices meet at the centre of enlargement.

Quick Check

A shape has area 8 cm². It is enlarged by scale factor 2. What is the new area?

6 Combinations and Describing Transformations Higher Skill Year 11

Example: Reflect in \(x = 1\), then reflect in \(x = 4\) (parallel mirrors 3 units apart). The combined effect is a translation of 6 units — not two reflections anymore.

You can do one transformation after another. Useful facts:

  • Two reflections in parallel lines (distance \(d\) apart) = translation by \(2d\) perpendicular to the lines.
  • Two reflections in lines that cross (angle \(\alpha\) between them) = rotation by \(2\alpha\) about the crossing point.
  • A transformation followed by its reverse = back to the start (identity).
  • Doing twice: reflections and 180° rotations return the shape to the original.

In exams: Apply transformations in order, one step at a time. If asked for a single equivalent transformation, work through the steps carefully.

7 Transformations — Quick Reference All Exam Boards Year 10 Revision Card

Use this as a revision card before a test. Pick the transformation type first, then use the matching rule from the card below.

Transformations — Quick Reference

Reflections

  • \(x\)-axis: \((x,y) \to (x,-y)\)
  • \(y\)-axis: \((x,y) \to (-x,y)\)
  • \(y=x\): \((x,y) \to (y,x)\)
  • \(y=-x\): \((x,y) \to (-y,-x)\)
  • Line \(x=k\): \((x,y) \to (2k-x,y)\)

Rotations (about origin)

  • 90° CW: \((x,y) \to (y,-x)\)
  • 90° ACW: \((x,y) \to (-y,x)\)
  • 180°: \((x,y) \to (-x,-y)\)

Translation & enlargement

  • Vector \((a,b)\): \((x,y) \to (x+a,y+b)\)
  • Enlargement SF \(k\) about origin: \((kx,ky)\)
  • Area scale factor = \(k^2\)

Combinations

  • Two parallel reflections → translation
  • Two reflections in intersecting lines → rotation
