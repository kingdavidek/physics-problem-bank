1 Three Ways of Writing the Same Value All Exam Boards Year 7

Fractions, decimals and percentages all mean the same thing: part of a whole. They're just three different ways of writing the same value.

For example: \(\frac{1}{2}\) = 0.5 = 50%. All three say exactly the same thing — "half".

The skill you need is converting confidently between all three forms. This page shows you every route, one at a time.

Solid arrows = covered in this lesson. Each section below takes you through one conversion step at a time.

Key pairs to memorise: These come up in almost every exam. Learn them now and save time later.

\(\frac{1}{2}\) = 0.5 = 50%
\(\frac{1}{4}\) = 0.25 = 25%
\(\frac{3}{4}\) = 0.75 = 75%
\(\frac{1}{5}\) = 0.2 = 20%
\(\frac{1}{10}\) = 0.1 = 10%
\(\frac{1}{3}\) ≈ 0.333 = 33.3%
2 Decimal → Percentage All Exam Boards Year 7

Converting a decimal to a percentage is the easiest conversion. Just multiply by 100 and add a % sign. That's it.

Why 100? Because "per cent" means "out of 100", so multiplying by 100 tells you how many parts per hundred.

Examples — try each one before reading the answer:

  • 0.29 × 100 = 29%
  • 0.375 × 100 = 37.5%
  • 1.25 × 100 = 125%  (more than 100% is fine — it means more than the whole)
  • 0.04 × 100 = 4%

The shortcut: multiplying by 100 is the same as moving the decimal point two places to the right.

0.375 → 037.5 → 37.5%

3 Percentage → Decimal All Exam Boards Year 7

Going the other way: to convert a percentage to a decimal, divide by 100. Move the decimal point two places to the left.

Examples:

  • 18% ÷ 100 = 0.18
  • 4% ÷ 100 = 0.04  (a small %, so the decimal is small too — makes sense!)
  • 125% ÷ 100 = 1.25
  • 7.5% ÷ 100 = 0.075

Why does this matter? Decimal multipliers are the key tool for percentage calculations in GCSE. For example, "increase by 18%" uses the multiplier 1.18, and "decrease by 18%" uses 0.82. You can't use these without being able to convert percentages to decimals fluently.

Tip: Percentage → Decimal is the reverse of Decimal → Percentage. One divides by 100, the other multiplies. If in doubt, check: 18% → 0.18 → 18%. ✓

4 Decimal → Fraction All Exam Boards Year 7

Use place value to write the decimal as a fraction, then simplify. The denominator (bottom number) is always a power of 10 — use 10 for one decimal place, 100 for two, 1000 for three.

Example: Convert 0.3 to a fraction.

  • One decimal place → denominator is 10
  • 0.3 = \(\frac{3}{10}\)  (already simplified — 3 and 10 share no common factors)

Example: Convert 0.25 to a fraction.

  • Two decimal places → denominator is 100
  • 0.25 = \(\frac{25}{100}\)
  • Simplify: HCF(25, 100) = 25 → divide both by 25 → \(\mathbf{\frac{1}{4}}\)

Example: Convert 0.12 to a fraction.

  • Two decimal places → \(\frac{12}{100}\)
  • HCF(12, 100) = 4 → divide both by 4 → \(\mathbf{\frac{3}{25}}\)

Use place value to write the decimal as a fraction over 10, 100, 1000 and so on, then simplify if possible.

⚠️ Always simplify! Leaving your answer as 25/100 when it should be 1/4 will cost you a mark in the exam.

5 Fraction → Decimal All Exam Boards Year 8

A fraction is just a division sum in disguise. To convert a fraction to a decimal, divide the top number by the bottom number.

\(\frac{\text{numerator}}{\text{denominator}} = \text{numerator} \div \text{denominator}\)

Examples:

  • \(\frac{3}{8}\) = 3 ÷ 8 = 0.375
  • \(\frac{1}{4}\) = 1 ÷ 4 = 0.25
  • \(\frac{2}{5}\) = 2 ÷ 5 = 0.4
  • \(\frac{5}{8}\) = 5 ÷ 8 = 0.625

Shortcut for "nice" denominators: If the denominator divides into a power of 10, you can make an equivalent fraction first.

Example: \(\frac{7}{20}\) — multiply top and bottom by 5 to get \(\frac{35}{100}\) = 0.35. Much faster than long division!

Convert a fraction to a decimal by dividing the numerator by the denominator.

6 Percentage → Fraction All Exam Boards Year 8

"Per cent" literally means per hundred. So any percentage is already a fraction with 100 on the bottom — you just need to write it down and simplify.

Example: Write 65% as a fraction.

  • 65% = \(\frac{65}{100}\)
  • HCF(65, 100) = 5 → divide both by 5
  • = \(\mathbf{\frac{13}{20}}\)

More examples:

  • 23% = \(\frac{23}{100}\)  (23 and 100 share no common factor, so it's already simplified)
  • 150% = \(\frac{150}{100} = \frac{3}{2}\)  (improper fraction — that's fine!)
  • 40% = \(\frac{40}{100} = \frac{2}{5}\)

Write the percentage over 100, then simplify.

7 Fraction → Percentage All Exam Boards Year 8

To convert a fraction to a percentage, use the two-step route you already know: fraction → decimal → percentage.

  1. Divide the numerator by the denominator (fraction → decimal)
  2. Multiply by 100 (decimal → percentage)

Examples:

  • \(\frac{3}{8}\) = 3 ÷ 8 = 0.375 → 0.375 × 100 = 37.5%
  • \(\frac{1}{5}\) = 1 ÷ 5 = 0.2 → 0.2 × 100 = 20%
  • \(\frac{3}{4}\) = 3 ÷ 4 = 0.75 → 0.75 × 100 = 75%

One-step shortcut: just multiply the fraction by 100.

\(\frac{3}{8} \times 100 = \frac{300}{8} = 37.5\%\)

First convert the fraction to a decimal, then multiply by 100.

8 Recurring Decimals → Fractions All Exam Boards Year 10 Higher

Some decimals go on forever with a repeating pattern — like 0.333… or 0.181818…. These are called recurring decimals. We write a dot over the repeating digits to show this: \(0.\dot{3}\) or \(0.\dot{1}\dot{8}\).

At Higher tier, you need to convert them to fractions using an algebra trick. Here's how:

Example: Write \(0.\dot{7}\) (= 0.7777…) as a fraction.

  • Let \(x = 0.7777\ldots\)
  • Multiply by 10: \(10x = 7.7777\ldots\)
  • Subtract: \(10x - x = 7.7777\ldots - 0.7777\ldots\)
  • \(9x = 7\)
  • \(x = \dfrac{7}{9}\)

Example: Write \(0.\dot{1}\dot{8}\) (= 0.181818…) as a fraction.

  • Let \(x = 0.181818\ldots\)
  • The repeating block "18" has 2 digits → multiply by 100: \(100x = 18.181818\ldots\)
  • Subtract: \(100x - x = 18\) → \(99x = 18\) → \(x = \dfrac{18}{99} = \dfrac{2}{11}\)

Some decimals repeat forever, like 0.333... or 0.181818.... At Higher tier you may be asked to convert them into fractions using algebra.

9 FDP Conversions — Quick Reference All Exam Boards Year 9 Revision Card

FDP Conversions — Quick Reference

Decimal conversions

  • Decimal → %: multiply by 100
  • % → decimal: divide by 100
  • Decimal → fraction: use place value, simplify
  • Fraction → decimal: top ÷ bottom

Fraction & percentage

  • % → fraction: write over 100, simplify
  • Fraction → %: divide, then × 100
  • Recurring decimal → fraction: algebra method
  • Always simplify fractions fully

Must-know equivalences

  • \(\frac12 = 0.5 = 50\%\)  ·  \(\frac14 = 0.25 = 25\%\)  ·  \(\frac34 = 0.75 = 75\%\)
  • \(\frac15 = 0.2 = 20\%\)  ·  \(\frac18 = 0.125 = 12.5\%\)
