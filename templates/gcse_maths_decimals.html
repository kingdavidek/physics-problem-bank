Decimals

AQA GCSE — Number (N2, N3, N10)

Place Value in Decimals

Every digit in a decimal number has a place value. Moving left multiplies by 10; moving right divides by 10.

HundredsTensUnits · TenthsHundredthsThousandths
1 4 7 · 3 0 6

So 147.306 means 1 hundred, 4 tens, 7 units, 3 tenths, 0 hundredths, 6 thousandths.

Ordering Decimals

Compare digits column by column, starting from the left. Never compare by length — 0.7 is greater than 0.35 even though 35 > 7 as integers.

  • To order 0.3, 0.35, 0.309: write them all to the same number of decimal places → 0.300, 0.350, 0.309
  • Ordering smallest to largest: 0.300, 0.309, 0.350 → i.e. 0.3, 0.309, 0.35

Adding and Subtracting Decimals

Line up the decimal points, then add or subtract as normal. Fill in gaps with zeros to avoid errors.

  14.72
+  3.08
------
  17.80

For subtraction, borrow across the decimal point exactly as you would with whole numbers.

Multiplying Decimals

Ignore the decimal points, multiply as integers, then count the total number of decimal places in both original numbers and put the point back.

Example: 2.4 × 1.3

  • 24 × 13 = 312
  • 2.4 has 1 d.p., 1.3 has 1 d.p. → total 2 d.p.
  • Answer: 3.12

Multiplying by powers of 10: move the decimal point right (× 10, × 100, × 1000) or left (÷ 10, ÷ 100, ÷ 1000).

  • 3.47 × 100 = 347
  • 52.6 ÷ 1000 = 0.0526

Dividing Decimals

If the divisor is a decimal, multiply both numbers by a power of 10 to make the divisor a whole number first.

Example: 4.8 ÷ 0.6

  • Multiply both by 10: 48 ÷ 6 = 8

Example: 2.94 ÷ 0.07

  • Multiply both by 100: 294 ÷ 7 = 42

Rounding Decimals

Look at the digit after the place you're rounding to. If it is 5 or more, round up; otherwise, round down.

  • 3.746 to 2 d.p. → look at 3rd d.p. = 6 ≥ 5 → round up → 3.75
  • 8.342 to 1 d.p. → look at 2nd d.p. = 4 < 5 → round down → 8.3
  • 5.996 to 2 d.p. → look at 3rd d.p. = 6 ≥ 5 → 6.00 (carry propagates!)

Fractions, Decimals and Percentages

These are three ways to express the same value. Key conversions to memorise:

FractionDecimalPercentage
1/20.550%
1/40.2525%
3/40.7575%
1/50.220%
1/80.12512.5%
1/30.333…33.3…%

Fraction → Decimal: divide numerator by denominator.
Decimal → Percentage: multiply by 100.
Percentage → Decimal: divide by 100.

Recurring Decimals (Higher)

A recurring decimal repeats a digit or block of digits forever. Use dot notation: 0.3̇ = 0.333…, 0.1̇4̇ = 0.141414…

To convert to a fraction, use the algebraic method:

Example: Convert 0.363636… to a fraction

  • Let x = 0.363636…
  • 100x = 36.363636…
  • 100x − x = 36 → 99x = 36
  • x = 36/99 = 4/11
